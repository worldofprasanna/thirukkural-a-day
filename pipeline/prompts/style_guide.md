# Visual style guide — Thirukkural kids' comics

This is a **hard constraint**, not a suggestion. Every panel must read as part of the same picture book. Style drift breaks the series.

## Style

Simplified Madhubani-inspired Indian folk art, softened and brightened for young children. Think of a picture book for four-year-olds, not a museum piece. Warm and inviting, never somber.

## Figures

- Flat, two-dimensional figures. No Western-style 3D shading or photorealism.
- Large, expressive almond-shaped eyes — kids should feel the characters' emotions at a glance.
- Rounded, soft facial features. Gentle smiles or clear single emotions (curious, surprised, thoughtful). No harsh or frightening expressions.
- Proportions are slightly stylised (larger heads than realistic) but readable as real people.
- Thin, deliberate black outlines on all figures and major objects.

## Palette

Earthy, traditional, limited:

- Turmeric yellow
- Indigo / deep blue
- Terracotta / brick red
- Forest green
- Warm cream (background)
- Soft black (outlines)

No neons, no gradients, no pastels. Colours are flat fills. A touch of white highlight on eyes is allowed; nothing else.

## Motifs

Decorative motifs (dots, small florals, vine borders, kolam patterns) are welcome but **sparingly** — this is a kids' illustration, not an ornate ritual piece. Let characters and action breathe. Avoid dense geometric backgrounds that compete with the scene.

## Setting

Tamil / South Indian village life: tiled-roof houses, neem trees, banana plants, courtyards with kolam at the doorstep, clay pots, bullock carts, brass lamps. Dress: veshti/dhoti, pavadai-sattai, sari, simple kurta. Never anachronistic Western clothing or architecture.

## Composition

- One clear subject per panel. The beat of the story should read instantly.
- Medium shots preferred; avoid extreme close-ups or wide establishing shots — the character's action should be the focus.
- No text, captions, speech bubbles, or signatures anywhere in the image.

## What to avoid

- Generic "Indian art" pastiche (Rajput miniatures, Tanjore gold-leaf, Warli stick figures — all wrong for this series).
- Dark / moody / fearful atmospheres.
- Realistic human proportions and shading.
- Modern urban settings or clothing.
- Any text rendered in the image.
